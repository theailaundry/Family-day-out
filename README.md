# Family Day Out Planner

A little helper that plans a day out with your kids.

You tell it your town, the day, and your kids' ages. It checks the weather, finds what's on that day, and gives you a few good plans. You read it in a minute and go.

It works with **Claude** (best) and with **ChatGPT** too.

You do not need to know anything about computers to use this. Just follow the steps. If you can fill in a form online, you can do this.

---

## What's in here

There are two files. Which one you use depends on your app.

- `family-day-out-planner.skill` → the ready-to-install skill, for **Claude**, and for **ChatGPT** on a paid work plan.
- `SKILL.md` → the plain-text instructions, for pasting into a ChatGPT Project or a free chat.

---

## Step 1: Download the file

You do **not** need a GitHub account. It is free and nothing signs you up.

1. Look near the top of this page for a green button that says **Code**. Click it.
2. A small menu opens. Click **Download ZIP**.
3. A file lands in your Downloads folder. Double-click it to open it (this is called "unzipping"). You now have a normal folder with the files inside.

That's it. The download is done.

> Just want one file? Click the file name in the list above (for example `SKILL.md`), then look for the small **Download** button (a downward arrow) on the right. Click it.

---

## Step 2: Use it

Pick the one you use. You only need to do this once.

### Option A: Claude (recommended)

This works in **Claude Cowork**, the desktop app version of Claude that can use files.

1. Open the Claude app.
2. Find the file `family-day-out-planner.skill` in your downloaded folder.
3. Drag that file into the Claude chat window, or use the attach (paperclip) button to add it.
4. Claude will offer a **Save skill** button. Click it.
5. Done. It is now installed.

To use it, just type something like:

> What should we do with the kids on Saturday near Amsterdam? They are 3 and 9.

Claude takes it from there.

### Option B: ChatGPT

ChatGPT now has skills too, but only on a **paid work plan** (Business, Enterprise, Edu, and similar), and only on the **website**, not the app yet. Pick the way that matches what you have.

**If you have the Skills button (paid work plan):**

1. Open ChatGPT on the web (not the app).
2. Click your profile icon (top right), then **Skills**.
3. Click **New skill**, then **Upload from your computer**.
4. Choose the `family-day-out-planner.skill` file you downloaded.
5. ChatGPT checks it. It's usually ready right away.

Then just ask, for example: *"Something to do with the kids Saturday near Amsterdam, ages 3 and 9."*

> If ChatGPT won't accept the file, rename it so it ends in `.zip` instead of `.skill` (it's the same file) and upload again. On Enterprise or Edu, an admin may need to switch Skills on first.

**No Skills button? Use a Project instead (any paid plan):**

1. Open the file `SKILL.md` (right-click it, open with TextEdit, Notepad, or any text app). Select everything and copy it.
2. In ChatGPT, make a new **Project**.
3. Paste the text into the project instructions box.
4. From now on, just chat inside that Project and ask. You only paste once.

**On free ChatGPT?** You can still use it. Start a new chat, paste the contents of `SKILL.md`, press enter, then type your request on the next line.

## Step 3: Ask it for a day out

Whichever one you use, ask in plain words. Tell it three things:

1. **Where** you are (your town or city).
2. **When** (today, Saturday, a date).
3. **Your kids' ages.**

Example:

> Something to do with the kids tomorrow near Rotterdam, ages 2 and 8, we can drive up to an hour.

If you forget something, it will ask you once, then get to work.

---

## What you get back

A short plan you can read in under a minute:

- The weather and anything important (like a closed beach).
- **Events on that day** first, because if you miss them they are gone.
- **Places that are always open** next, like museums and zoos.
- One **surprise** spot you probably haven't heard of.
- A few handy notes (parking, what to book, age limits).

---

## Good to know

- It plans **one day at a time**. Ask again for a different day.
- Opening times and prices can change. It checks what it can, but for anything important, look at the place's own website before you drive.
- It works best in towns and cities with lots going on. In quiet areas you'll get fewer events and more regular places.

---

## What is a "skill"?

A skill is just a set of instructions you give the AI once. After that, the AI follows them every time you ask, like a recipe it cooks from. You don't need to set it up again.

Made for parents who want a good day out without spending the morning searching. Enjoy it.
